# Interview Preparation Checklist

## Before the Interview

### Research
- [ ] Read the company's engineering blog (last 3-5 posts)
- [ ] Understand the product — sign up and use it if possible
- [ ] Check recent news: funding rounds, acquisitions, product launches
- [ ] Look up your interviewers on LinkedIn — find common ground
- [ ] Review the tech stack from job description and Glassdoor reviews
- [ ] Read recent Glassdoor interview reviews for the same role

### Technical Prep
- [ ] Review core data structures: arrays, linked lists, trees, graphs, hash maps, heaps
- [ ] Practice 2-3 problems per pattern: sliding window, two pointers, BFS/DFS, DP, backtracking
- [ ] Prepare 2 system design problems end-to-end (for mid/senior)
- [ ] Review your own projects — be ready to deep dive on architecture decisions
- [ ] Practice coding on a whiteboard or shared editor (not your IDE)
- [ ] Time yourself: 20 min for medium problems, 35 min for hard

### Behavioral Prep
- [ ] Prepare 8-10 STAR stories covering:
  - A time you led a project
  - A technical disagreement and how you resolved it
  - A failure and what you learned
  - Working under tight deadlines
  - Mentoring or helping a teammate
  - Dealing with ambiguity
  - A project you're most proud of
  - A time you had to make a tradeoff
- [ ] Practice saying stories out loud (2-3 minutes each)
- [ ] Prepare 5+ thoughtful questions for each interviewer

### Logistics
- [ ] Confirm date, time, and timezone
- [ ] Test your camera, mic, and internet (for virtual)
- [ ] Have a backup plan (phone hotspot, alternative device)
- [ ] Prepare a quiet, well-lit space with a clean background
- [ ] Have water and a notepad ready

## During the Interview

### Coding Rounds
1. **Clarify** — restate the problem, ask about edge cases, input constraints
2. **Plan** — talk through your approach before coding
3. **Code** — write clean code with meaningful variable names
4. **Test** — walk through your code with an example, then edge cases
5. **Optimize** — discuss time/space complexity, mention potential improvements

### System Design Rounds
1. **Requirements** — functional and non-functional (latency, scale, availability)
2. **Estimations** — back-of-envelope: QPS, storage, bandwidth
3. **High-level design** — draw the core components and data flow
4. **Deep dive** — pick 1-2 components to detail (the interviewer will guide you)
5. **Tradeoffs** — discuss why you chose this approach over alternatives

### Behavioral Rounds
- Answer in STAR format: Situation, Task, Action, Result
- Keep answers to 2-3 minutes
- Focus on YOUR contribution, not the team's
- End with what you learned or would do differently

## After the Interview

- [ ] Send a thank-you email within 24 hours
- [ ] Note what went well and what to improve
- [ ] Follow up if you haven't heard back in the stated timeline
- [ ] If rejected, ask for feedback (they may or may not provide it)
