# Beating the ATS (Applicant Tracking System)

## What is ATS?

Most companies use Applicant Tracking Systems to filter resumes before a human ever sees them. Common systems include Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, iCIMS, and Taleo. Understanding how they work gives you a significant advantage.

## How ATS Scoring Works

1. **Keyword matching** — ATS looks for exact matches of skills, tools, and qualifications from the job description
2. **Section parsing** — it tries to identify Education, Experience, and Skills sections by their headers
3. **Date parsing** — it reads employment dates to calculate experience length
4. **Ranking** — candidates are scored and ranked; recruiters typically review the top 20-50%

## Optimization Strategies

### Keywords
- Read the job description carefully and identify **required** vs **preferred** qualifications
- Include required keywords verbatim in your resume (don't paraphrase "React" as "React.js" unless both appear)
- Place the most important keywords in your Skills section AND in experience bullets
- Use both the acronym and full name: "Amazon Web Services (AWS)"

### Format
- Use a **single-column layout** — multi-column layouts break parsing
- Standard fonts: Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, or Helvetica
- No text boxes, images, or graphics
- Use standard bullet characters (-, *)
- Section headers should be exactly: **Experience**, **Education**, **Skills**, **Projects**
- Don't put critical information in headers or footers

### File Format
- Submit as **PDF** unless the application specifically asks for .docx
- Name your file: `FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf`
- Make sure the PDF is text-selectable (not a scanned image)

### Common ATS Failures
- Using "Responsibilities included:" instead of action verbs
- Fancy templates from Canva or similar tools
- Two-column layouts that merge content in parsing
- Tables for formatting (ATS reads them row-by-row, not column-by-column)
- Special characters or unicode symbols
- Non-standard section headers like "Where I've Made Impact"

## Testing Your Resume

1. **Copy-paste test** — paste your PDF into a plain text editor. If it's readable and in order, ATS can probably parse it
2. **Keyword check** — highlight every keyword from the job description that appears in your resume. Aim for 80%+ coverage of required skills
3. **Jobscan** (jobscan.co) — free tool that scores your resume against a job description
